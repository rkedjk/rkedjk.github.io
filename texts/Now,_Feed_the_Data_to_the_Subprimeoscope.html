Getty Images Here are some other factors: THE HOUSING MARKET We are clearly in the “contraction” phase of an extended period of inappropriately robust residential real estate price appreciation. This is widely seen as “bad,” and explains the uptick in men walking around in barrels. In fact, October sales of existing homes sank by one-fifth, with sales of make-believe homes faring little better. Additionally, home prices are set to fall nationwide this year. This hasn’t happened since 1933, meaning that 2008 will be the first time housing costs will decrease in color. The subprime loan crisis has only exacerbated  in lay terms, “more baddened”  the trend, which runs particularly high in minority neighborhoods, as well as certain “transitional” areas on the verge of becoming “so over.” Such news obviously encourages the bears out there; should you encounter one, call animal control and stay in your home, existing or otherwise. Supplementary recession delta: +1.5 CONSUMER SPENDING Time was when no matter what happened to the United States economy, Joe and Julie Consumer were its saviors. Taking advantage of low interest rates, they used their house as an A.T.M., their garage as an ornate neoclassical lobby with chained pens, and the gazebo out back as an armored safe-deposit vault. The money flowed only quicker after the bitter divorce between Joe and his first wife, the former Jill Consumer, née Jillian Shoppingspree, now back to using her maiden name, thank you very much; lawyers, therapists, physical therapists, and, as Joe and Jill competed for the let’s-be-honest already nearly imperceptible affections of an increasingly sullen preteen Joe Jr., toy and candy and sporting goods stores. Everyone made out as the Consumers kept spending like drunken sailors. (Drunken sailors, notably, were seen during the last Fleet Week buying low-risk bonds. In hindsight, something was up.)

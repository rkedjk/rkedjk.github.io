The Fed’s G.D.P. Forecast, in Context

https://economix.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/02/16/the-feds-g-d-p-forecast-in-context/

Minutes from the Federal Reserve’s Federal Open Market Committee meeting released today revealed a somewhat more upbeat set of monetary policy makers, with a forecast for output growth of 3.4 to 3.9 percent this year. That’s slightly higher than their last projection of 3 to 3.6 percent, released in November. But it’s still not great. In fact, it’s not much better than the average annual growth rate of the last 80 years: Bureau of Economic Analysis, via Haver Analytics …whereas we need something way, way faster than average growth to return our economy to a state that feels healthy, considering how quickly the economy shrank during the Great Recession.

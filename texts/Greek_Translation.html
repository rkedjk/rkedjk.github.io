FT Alphaville summarizes the Greek deal; if you aren’t confused, you aren’t paying attention. For my part, I think of this as a version of the Brady plans adopted by Latin American nations at the end of the 1980s. There too creditors were given a choice between bailing further in or accepting a reduction in principal. The difference here is that the deal is apparently all voluntary; creditors can simply stay put. Presumably the implicit threat is that if they don’t buy into the deal, there may still be a default that will leave them in the cold. Also, Latin debt levels and the required austerity were much lower than those in Greece. And calculating the actual implied debt relief is beyond me. I guess this still doesn’t look anything like adequate to me; nonetheless, markets are rallying big. Maybe they were pricing in the risk of a breakdown right away.

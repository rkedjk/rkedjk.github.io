Paying for the Welfare State Without Raising Taxes by Roger E.A. Farmer

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/national-treasuries-social-care-funds-by-roger-farmer-2019-06

I have big doubts about Farmer’s arguments. First, the UK (where I live) has had trillions of pounds worth of “unfunded” social security costs since WWII: i.e. the costs of social security each year is paid for by taxpayers in that year. The sky keeps refusing to fall in in the UK. Second, the REASON government can borrow at a lower rate than anyone else stems from government’s dictatorial powers: that is, as everyone knows, governments never have a problem repaying loans denominated in their own currency because they can simply grab whatever money they need off taxpayers, or just print money. The latter is not a genuine free market transaction, which in turn means that Farmer’s method of comparing costs of different options is flawed. Third, on the not unreasonable and simplifying assumption that each dollar of stock market investment done by government under Farmer’s scheme displaces a dollar’s worth of investment done by some private individual, any profits from that investment simply amounts to a transfer from private individuals to the state. Why not stick to the traditional method of transferring dollars from private individuals to the state, i.e. tax? Strikes me that under the latter system, the exact costs to private individuals are more transparent and easy to quantify.

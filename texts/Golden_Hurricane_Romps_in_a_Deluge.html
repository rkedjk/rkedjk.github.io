Golden Hurricane Romps in a Deluge

https://www.nytimes.com/2009/01/07/sports/ncaafootball/07bowl.html?partner=rss&emc=rss

MOBILE, Ala. (AP)  Tarrion Adams rushed for 207 yards and 3 touchdowns as Tulsa defeated No. 23 Ball State, 45-13, in a soggy GMAC Bowl on Tuesday night. The Golden Hurricane (11-3) finished with a team record for victories. Adams passed Micheal Gunter to become Tulsa’s career rushing leader, and Tulsa kicker Jarod Tracy became the team’s career scoring leader. Tulsa had 439 rushing yards and 632 total yards during a rain that first formed only puddles and then covered nearly the entire field during a second-half deluge. David Johnson passed for 193 yards and 3 touchdowns. The freshman receiver Damaris Johnson supplied many of the big plays for Tulsa’s no-huddle offense. He had 135 receiving yards, 76 yards rushing  including a tackle-breaking 62-yard run in the fourth quarter  and he returned three kicks for 63 yards.

Fixing America’s Fiscal Problem by Martin Feldstein

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/us-obama-romney-entitlements-cliff-by-martin-feldstein

In the BIS paper 361,“Long-term fiscal sustainability in major economies” by Alan J Auerbach, Discussion Comments by Pier Carlo Padoan, Paul van den Noord and Ray Barrell , we have the notings from Auerbach and Gale (2011) who estimates "a federal-government fiscal gap for the United States at between 4 and 10 percent of GDP over the infinite time horizon (ie for T → ∞), and between 3 and 6 percent through 2060" if the current debt to GDP ratio is kept the same. In fact the current debt to GDP ratio is likely to increase to 89% of the GDP by the end of this decade. Added to this is the dimension that the unfunded medicare liabilities are in the region of $40 Trillion. Adjustments to these liabilities on one hand and reducing government spending when there is a shift in the demographics is a far more complex problem than we could do justice in a small article as attempted by Feldstein. By postponing to deal with the problem (six months could well be for four years) while at the same time attempting to limit the role of government, we are in effect shifting the onus of mending fiscal rectitude to those forces of market participation who have no way of influencing the outcome while many ways of gaining from the abstinence in the short and medium term.

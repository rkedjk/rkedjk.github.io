Show paragraph "...the wealthy would be well advised to indulge less in conspicious consumption" This sounds all well and good, but it's rather meaningless given the empirical evidence. Moreover, I feel that Bhagwatti is attacking a Straw Man, just as much as I feel that some of those on the left (full disclosure: I am traditionally leftist) misconstrue the growth argument to suit short sighted political or ideological objectives. Neither side of the polemic really add anything of substance other than repeating tired arguments about the effectiveness and efficacy of various growth models. I'm well aware of the importance, don't get me wrong, but development is much more complicated to be reduced to such inanities every time the subject is brought forward. Better to look at what works best for the particular situation at hand. To see how implementation, or lack thereof, of specific policies is negatively impacting those in the bottom quintiles. And to rectify such issues in dialogue with those most affected by them.

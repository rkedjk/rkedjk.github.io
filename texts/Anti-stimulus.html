So stimulus has been fading out fast; in effect, we’re pursuing a contractionary fiscal policy. Meanwhile, the underlying source of our slump, the high level of consumer debt inherited largely from the housing bubble, has declined only slightly. And quelle surprise, as Yves Smith would say, the economy is sputtering. If you do the 1937 thing, you shouldn’t be surprised at getting the 1937 result.

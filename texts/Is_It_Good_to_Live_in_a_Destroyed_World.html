Is It Good to Live in a Destroyed World?

https://krugman.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/09/28/is-it-good-to-live-in-a-destroyed-world/

On this blog and elsewhere, I often see assertions that America prospered after the Depression because our competitors were in a state of ruin. That’s just not right. First of all, trade was a minor factor in the US economy both before and after the war, with both imports and exports a much smaller share of GDP than they are now. Second, while it’s true that the war had largely destroyed our overseas competitors, it had also largely destroyed our overseas customers — because by and large these were the same thing. Here’s the data on net exports as a share of GDP, which is what matters for overall demand. It was a small number before the war, and was back to being a small number by around 1950. There was a bulge for a few years in the late 40s. What was that about? The Marshall Plan, which temporarily allowed ruined economies to buy more from us — in effect, a form of fiscal stimulus. In general, devastation is bad for business.

About prestige and status I heard once one random student in Switzerland say that the most prestigious professions are lawyer and medical doctor. Some time later, in a book about the sayings of Ramakrishna, i noticed with some surprise a saying that was approximately "the people that are furthest away from God are the lawyers, the doctors and the bankers". In the footnote the translators interpretation was that it was because those professions generate the strongest egos (as a sense of self-importance), the people practicing those professions are most unlikely to perceive themselves as part of the whole, in other words are least enabled to transcend their personal self to see unity underneath duality. On a zoological level, generally speaking the higher our social status, be it through income our function, the higher our chances of survival will be as our social environment finds more potential rewards in protecting us. In Venezuela, given the apparent ethnic melting pot, meritocracy might have needed to be complemented with some measures of affirmative action if for some reasons, be it genetic or cultural, the population ended up seeing mostly white europeans staff the meritocratic structures.

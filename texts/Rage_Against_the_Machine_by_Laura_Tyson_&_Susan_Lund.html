Rage Against the Machine? by Laura Tyson & Susan Lund

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/automation-displaced-workers-transition-by-laura-tyson-and-susan-lund-2017-12

This article is either delusional - or (sinisterly) disingenuous. If the authors believe what they write, I would challenge them to point me to a couple of dozen professions that they think will remain human-only preserves over the next two decades. I am willing to bet that for every profession they come up with, like Priest, Teacher and so on, I can point to at least ten that will become *fully* automated. "But history and economic theory suggest that fears about technological unemployment, a term coined by John Maynard Keynes nearly a century ago, are misplaced." This is rot - history says the future carries on as in the past - until it doesn't. As for economic theory, just don't even go there, for fear of economists being laughed out of court. The truth is there we are approaching the end of the road for the type of mass employment that the last industrial revolution created. There is no more road. If you think other types of work will be created that will provide work for billions then I challenge you - point me to those professions - and I will point you right back at those same professions being also automated in short order. "These trends must be reversed. Lifelong learning needs to become a reality." Sure, but this will offer a stay of execution only and that for a couple of decades tops. What then? A final challenge, on the numbers quoted - "75-375 million workers" and other figures quoted are hokum - or if you prefer, economic theory. There is evidence that almost 30% of all work around *right now* is automatable, but hasn't been automated for various reasons, and this is provided as argument that automation will unfold at a linear leisurely pace into the future. I disagree. Technology-driven change is like pulling on a very large brick with a rubber-band: for a long time nothing seems to be happening - and then, just when you expect it to start creeping towards you, it instead wizzes through the air - and smashes you in the face. Regarding large-scale Automation, it's already started and we stand, right now, on the edge of a startlingly fast ramp upwards. Once some businesses start to automate, others are forced to follow - or go bust. The speed of change will acclerate as professions like accountancy and law start to lose (very smart) workers - these type of people can and will retrain and move into still-viable IT related professions, speeding up the process. My fear is for the billions of dirt poor people in Africa and Asia. How do these people re-invent themselves? Gene-editing or eugenics? I'm not saying at all that humanity should go back to the horse and plough; for the start the option doesn't exist without creating ISIS type dystopias. But let no-one peddle the delusion that what cones next is anything other than chaotic, and likely catastrophic for the majority of humanity.

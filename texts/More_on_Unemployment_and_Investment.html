More on Unemployment and Investment

https://krugman.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/03/30/more-on-unemployment-and-investment/

To follow up on John Taylor: suppose we look only at nonresidential investment, and suppose that we think (as we should) that the causation runs mainly from unemployment — a proxy for excess capacity — to business investment, rather than the other way around. Then the data since 1990 look like this: BEA, BLS Investment is low as a share of GDP; well, that’s no surprise given how depressed the economy is. And if anything investment is a bit stronger than you might have expected from past behavior.

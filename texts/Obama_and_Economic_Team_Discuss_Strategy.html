Obama and Economic Team Discuss Strategy

https://www.nytimes.com/2010/08/26/business/26obama.html?partner=rss&emc=rss

Some private forecasters have reduced their growth projections partly because the administration’s stimulus plan is winding down and a polarized Congress shows little sign of doing more. Mr. Obama and Congress’s Democratic leaders have only a short time for action between lawmakers’ return and their departure again in October to campaign for re-election. And Republicans are emboldened in their opposition, as polls attesting to the public’s frustration with persistent high unemployment make the party increasingly hopeful of winning control of Congress in November. In any case, nothing the White House and Congress do would show results before the elections. Even so, the pressure to act could well intensify next week when Mr. Obama is back in Washington. His week will be dominated by foreign policy  Mr. Obama gives a prime-time address on the end of combat operations in Iraq on Tuesday and then hosts two days of Middle East peace talks  yet it will end that Friday with the release of unemployment data for August, which has Democrats braced for disappointing news. The administration’s main initiative now, which the president proposed at the start of the year, would provide additional tax cuts to small businesses and create a government-backed body to increase lending to them. It has been stalled by Republicans in the Senate. Also, Congress must act by the end of this year if the Bush-era income tax cuts are to be extended beyond their Dec. 31 expiration. Mr. Obama wants to extend the tax cuts for 98 percent of Americans, but the Republicans are insisting on extending all the tax cuts given the economy’s continued weakness. The fight over tax cuts for the wealthy is likely to dominate discussion in coming weeks, and the president and his economic and political teams have only begun to focus on their strategy.

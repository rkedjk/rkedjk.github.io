ataxingmatter: More on Estate Tax "reform" (read--"help for the uber-rich")

http://ataxingmatter.blogs.com/tax/2009/04/more-on-estate-tax-reform-readhelp-for-the-uberrich.html

I reported on the Senate's Lincoln-Kyl amendment, passed by 51-48, to weaken the scope of the estate tax for the benefit of the top one-quarter of one-percent, the "uber-rich", in last week's posting, "A Link Worth Noting: Can You Believe the Senate's Estate Tax Proposal?" The gist--where does the current Senate get these ideas, that in the midst of a gigantic economic recession, when trillions are already being committed to bail out Big Banks (and their wealthy shareholders) while ordinary Americans are facing excruciating job losses and shrinking pocketbooks from the loss of home values, the Senate comes up with another idea for giveaways for the uber-wealthy. I quoted the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities, which has put together some interesting numbers that reveal the clear way that this additional revenue reduction benefits the ultra-wealthy. Hard to think of a worse response..... Bob Greenstein agrees, in "Senate to Uber-Rich: 'Help is on the way'", Huffington Post, Apr. 13, 2009. He makes it clear. "Only a tiny fraction of estates will owe any estate tax in 2011 if 2009 law is extended--Only 0.25% of estates (1 out of every 400) will owe any estate tax in 2011." But under the proposal, those few "[e]states worth more than $20 million would get an average tax cut of $3.5 million." And he attacks the misleading propagandizing that has been the modus operandi of the 'coalition against fair taxes' (my name) from the beginning.

I don't necessarily endorse every item on the list, but it's a good place to start. Ezra Klein: 32 flavors of stimulus, ordered by deliciousness - 32 flavors of stimulus, ordered by deliciousness Leading the list of high-multiplier items is direct spending by the federal government, infrastructure aid to states and localities, other types of aid to states and localities, and transfer payments (think unemployment insurance and food stamps) to individuals. At the bottom of the list are corporate tax cuts, the new homeowner's tax credit and individual tax cuts. Of course, of that list, the only policy we've rushed to expand is the homeowner's tax credit, which turns out to be among the two worst approaches if you're worried about stimulus.

Hi Barry, Timing is everything, as they say. And now (*before* the next crash) is the best time to focus on improving macroprudential policy. There has never been a better time to institute a Tobin Tax in the developed nations. Billions of dollars would quickly accrue and could be used to (among other things) rescue wobbly banks, or used to buy them outright and resell them at some point to any solid bank for a fair market price. No doubt, other uses for these rapidly accruing funds would be found such as streamlining bank operations, increased online security (for customers) and payments systems (for banking systems) and to protect banks from organized crime syndicates. And as Prof. Eichengreen has rightly pointed out, the following should be given fast-track and high priority: "So what should central bankers do instead? Ideally, they would develop a set of specially tailored financial tools. For example, raising banks’ capital requirements when credit is booming could restrain lending and strengthen banks’ cushion against losses, while setting ceilings on loan-to-value ratios could rein in exuberant property markets, thereby heading off excessive risks for borrowers and lenders." -- Barry Eichengreen Always great to read your thoughts, Professor.

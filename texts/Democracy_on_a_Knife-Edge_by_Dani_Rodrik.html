Democracy on a Knife-Edge by Dani Rodrik

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/why-liberal-democracy-is-both-rare-and-fragile-by-dani-rodrik-2019-10

In terms of corruption, it depends on how it is defined. Is political lobbying corruption ? Are political favours corruption ? Is giving one's offspring a head start (in the case of Hunter Biden, for example) corruption ? It is common in capitalistic democracies, such as the US, to have people cycling between government and private institutions. For example, the treasury and Goldman Sachs, the energy department and Exxon, the Pentagon and Raytheon. The officials making policies would therefore tend to favour private enterprises over the public good if their plan is to reenter the private sector. Is this corruption ? If all of these are allowed under the law, they are therefore legal, but are they ethical ? You can argue there is rampant corruption in China, because many of these activities that are illegal in China still happen, but they happen in the US as well, where they are legal. Political contributions are considered corruption in the UK, but under similar circumstances would be legal in the US. Therefore, arguably, countries with the stricter laws have more corruption than those that don't. Ironically, foreigners could be convicted of corruption through the Foreign Corrupt Practices law in the US, whereas what they do might be legal in their home countries. The opposite is obviously not true for US citizens, and even if US citizens break laws abroad, the US government usually try to prevent them from being tried in foreign courts.

Enough With the Moral Hazard Fears

https://www.newyorker.com/online/blogs/jamessurowiecki/2009/02/enough-with-the.html

Valleywag’s Owen Thomas offers up an excellent rant (that’s their word, not mine) against the moral-hazard fundamentalists who insist that bailouts are a bad idea because they’ll encourage bad behavior in the future. As I wrote a few weeks ago, there’s surprisingly little evidence that moral hazard has a meaningful effect on decisionmaking, and so far in this crisis far more damage has been done by policies that were designed to avoid moral hazard (like letting Lehman Brothers fail) than by those that supposedly encourage it. And Thomas gets at the key point, which is that this is a very odd time to be worrying about people taking too much risk. In fact, as he writes, the problem we’re facing right now is “too little risktaking, not too much.” People are so understandably worried about their jobs, their savings, and so on, that they’ve become far more risk averse than they normally would be. Bailouts may make people a little more likely to take a gamble. But at the moment, that’s precisely what the economy needs.

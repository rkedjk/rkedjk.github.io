Gas Costs Ease, Offering Hope on Economy

https://www.nytimes.com/2012/05/16/business/economy/consumer-prices-and-retail-sales-are-flat.html?partner=rss&emc=rss

WASHINGTON (AP) — Americans are starting to see some relief from higher gasoline prices, a change that could revive the economy in the months ahead. Consumer prices were flat in April, the Labor Department reported on Tuesday, largely because of a decline in gas prices. Cheaper gas may be combining with steady job growth to increase spending on more expensive items. Sales of autos, furniture and electronics all rose in April. And Americans spent more at restaurants and bars, generally a sign of confidence in the economy.

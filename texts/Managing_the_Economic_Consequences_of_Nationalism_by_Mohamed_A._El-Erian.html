I think sir you have made a sound analysis but in the end "populism" should not just be seen as code for extremism. Surely the truth of Brexit and other "populists" is that the EU is failing across the Continent and just because the UK may be able to salvage a relationship to minimise the two way devastation does not mean the EU can sink back into its torpor. Even to day we have the latest ramblings of Verhofstadt in the FT making bitter threats about the EU and a very messy cat in the bag scrap between those who want to ignore the Brexit vote and assume we can go on as we are and the millions of voters (like me) who voted OUT of the EU for sound economic and democratic reasons. In the end it was not the UK that changed, it was the EEC into the EU. The UK only ever wanted to be in a trading relationship. By all means we can co-operate on the bigger issues, but the blatant refusal of all politicians to allow the UK peoples to vote on subcontracting their democracy to the EU was until last year NEVER undertaken and the Brexit vote must be seen in that context. I also believe many other European countries have had their views similarly silenced and referenda repeated until the "correct" response has been received, usually followed by threats and lies about consequences. The EU cannot hope to have a future if the only way it can keep countries in the "club" is by threats and blackmail and by trying to destroy those countries that decide this is not a club that is successful; honourable; or sustainable or for them. In the end the "populist" view in the form of Brexit came about because politicians would not listen and the same can be said of Merkel not listening about unwanted mass migration; and the EU refusing to listen to the valid and genuine criticisms and concerns millions of us have about how they are running Europe. The EU cannot survive if it is simply the Hollande and Merkel Show, also German politicians need to tread extremely carefully in their insults about the UK, not only because a massive "populist" backlash against German imports will be an immediate and painful consequence, but also Germany has to resist being seen as running Europe, something it was unable to achieve through two world wars and now finds itself back in the firing line again. The EU has to stop finger wagging. Nation States are the repository of culture; language; history and identity if the EU seriously imagined that these valued features of who and what we are could be wiped away because Germany has a self loathing problem then they could not have misjudged the situation more. I am proud to be British as I am sure the French are proud to be French it is not yet a hanging offence to love your country... Nation States are the only way our democratic rights can be protected and with majority voting, the UK flagged up that EU nett donors were being skimmed of resources at the behest of those countries in the majority who cannot run stable economies. The EU is a supplicant organisation where the contributors are outnumbered by the claimants - this type of structure lacks the sustainability needed for the long term and by the UK leaving stability is in a very dark place. In my view, the EU would do well to knuckle down and thrash out a British deal, which creates minimal disruption to everyone, acknowledges the mass inward migration the UK has unequally borne, permits the management of migration and limits on unskilled migrants and welfare claiming - which had the Eu listened when Cameron came calling we could have avoided this nightmare. Now they have a second chance to save the day don't blow it. I believe that in return for financial support to the EU and a close working relationship within an EEA framework (with some unilateral deals within certain limits) the UK can be persuaded to remain an active part of Europe. The EU needs to demonstrate that it has the presence of mind, maturity and intellectual strength to cut a deal that turns disruption into minor ripples and FINALLY recognises the UK for what it is - a massive economy; a large finace centre; non participant in the Euro/Shengen and a country better within the European family than ostracised by it. Britain has done much for Europe and her dead are strewn across Europe in her defence, let's not forget that when the EU Elites sharpen their knives before our negotiations. Here is how Europe can demonstrate we were not wasting our time when we delivered Europe from authoritarianism. It would be extremely unwise for the EU to approach these negotiations with animosity and spite, if the British people feel they are being "punished" then the buying patterns of the UK will change overnight and everyone will suffer from that. What we need now are cool heads, flexibility and the EU to demonstrate it can keep countries in the EEA especially when to lose them completely could bring the house of cards down. The UK doesn't want this but it does want massive and real change - as do many other societies in the EU - the ball is actually in the EU's court can it repair the damage with the populists?

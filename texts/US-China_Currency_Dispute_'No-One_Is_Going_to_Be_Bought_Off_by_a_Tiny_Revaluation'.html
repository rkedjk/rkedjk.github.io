US-China Currency Dispute: 'No-One Is Going to Be Bought Off by a Tiny Revaluation'

US-China Currency Dispute 'No-One Is Going to Be Bought Off by a Tiny Revaluation' In the runup to the G-20 summit, China has tried to placate the US with a revaluation of its currency. But the move is not a real change of course, explains the German Marshall Fund's Andrew Small in a SPIEGEL ONLINE interview. He argues that the Chinese leadership is more concerned with deflecting external criticism than with the health of the global economy.

Is Politics Getting to the Fed? by Robert J. Barro

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/trump-powell-politics-fed-by-robert-j-barro-2019-07

A positive long term real rate is important if any long term retirement plans are to be funded. There is nothing, however, sacrosanct about 2.5% being the long term rate. The question, however, is a good one. What is the logic of cutting now when the economic data is strong? The answer perhaps is less pressure from Trump but rather that Powell and some of the other governors and some members of the Fed staff have conveniently convinced themselves that there is a significant wealth effect from equity market prices. Few economists and indeed few people in the financial services industry privately believe in any significant link between equity markets and the real economy, but on CNBC and Bloomberg this view is repeated frequently. I am suggesting that Powell is not significantly influenced by his own wealth and private equity background or a desire to appease Trump. Far more worryingly, he and some other parts of the Fed have uncritically accepted an unsupported economic view. This view had an impact on the Fed prior to Powell and only seems to be growing in its importance in interest rate setting.

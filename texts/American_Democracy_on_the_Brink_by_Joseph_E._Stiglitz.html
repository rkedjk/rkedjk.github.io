American Democracy on the Brink by Joseph E. Stiglitz

Joseph E. Stiglitz again plods beyond his ken. Thank goodness that his Far-Left-of-Center is not holding. Obama worked hard toward his goal of “fundamentally transforming” the America he and Stiglitz find so malodorous. Obama finagled every way within his overstretched reach, unimportant whether illegal or untrue, to unfasten the American Constitution from its Declared roots, to float it wherever his (and Stiglitz’s) progressive impulses might blow. Stiglitz upsets himself because Trump, duly elected as was Obama, might be able to select a justice of Trump’s choice, perhaps another Constitutional Conservative who may help right the badly left-listing American ship. Obama had his turn, appointing two Progressive (=Statist) justices, Sotomayor and Kagan, who with their Progressive colleagues on the Supreme Court Breyer and Ginsburg, still hang onto the inherently unstable Wilsonian obsession with a “living constitution”, whose recent predominance has turned the judicial branch, a once equal branch of the American tri-partite government (with the Presidency and Congress), into the hands-down most powerful branch. Stiglitz and Democrats (=Progressives) are uncorked that Trump might have a Constitutional Conservation judge replace the retiring Justice Kennedy. Justice Kennedy will not be remembered as Stiglitz’s “swing voter”, but as an emotional philosophical transcendentalist justice whose mental meanderings in his decisions were the airy bases (for example, “mystery of human life”) of many of his leftish decisions far from the Constitution which he swore to uphold. He was a Leftist that the Progressives on the Court could depend on for key issues. Stiglitz speaks of recent “divisive rulings”. If the rulings had been decided the other way, satisfactory to Stiglitz senses, Constitutionalists would have grumbled, again. But there would have been no lunatic howlings nor bodily threats that voters would by now be tiring of if such reactions weren’t verging on, even egging on, violence. Children must have their way. Progressives used to preach tolerance. But it is the tolerance of their agenda only. Progressives never ever quit on their agenda. Democrats used to have some decent members with good hearts and good heads who had America’s best interest at heart. Today they are a herd. Stiglitz’s notion of “impartial adjudicator” is upside down, just as is his classifying Conservatism as “extreme”. He might spend more time in America to learn see what the average man thinks of the so-called “impartial” decisions of the Progressive Court. Its “living constitution” decisions are oppose what Americans believe in, in cases such as abortion, homosexuality, religion, the Pledge of Allegiance, etc. Stiglitz worries about “tribal politics”, ordering people into categories that divide rather than unify. It is the Progressives who stick people into classes of race, class, wealth, sex, etc, for divide-trouble-and-conquer political leverage. Calling the skeletal tribes Americans might be unifying. The “constitution” of Stiglitz and his fellow Progressives is one that changes day-to-day, judge-by-federal judge. Judges and Supreme Court justices are funnily thought to be wise men and women. We see now a single wise-man -or-woman federal judge, of the 3000 federal judges, can defy Constitutional authority and stop the workings of the country. The nine Supreme Court justices can turn the whole of America on its ear almost permanently by its interpretations of the Constitution. No matter that they are not elected, these largely representatives of liberal schools (is there any other kind today?) like the one Stiglitz associates himself with. Would that Trump nominate a justice who once on the Supreme Court is wise enough to say, “This case is not our business. We cannot rule because the Constitution says nothing about it. Go to the Congress to have what you want made into law.” It is very queer that Stiglitz labels the Supreme Court “another instrument for advancing an extreme right-wing agenda, one that has subjected the United States to minority rule”. The Leftist extremists (the three women and Breyer) on the Court have long been bedfellows with the Stiglitzian Leftist Democrats, whose type has used the Court to bring about extreme fanciful readings of the Constitution (a sample: Griswald, Eisenstadt, Roe), doing with the Court what Democrats cannot do in Congress and at the ballet box. The liberal barely Constitutional decisions of Supreme Court decisions is a large part of why Americans are at war with each other. The Court is to blame for enacting as law too much, too soon, and too extreme of the high-speed Progressive wish list to digest. And the Progressives don’t have an endgame except to destroy America’s Constitution and replace it with thousands of Court decisions that no one can keep track of on the way to complete federal control over the states and individuals that brought American into existence and formed its limited government. The Supreme Court amends the Constitution with every decision, rather than Americans or Congress amending it according to the means set out in Article 5 of that very Constitution. Trump is rebuilding a “pillar” of America, one that Stiglitz doesn’t have in mind, one that Obama almost destroyed: respect—respect for America within and without, and respect for the American Constitution.

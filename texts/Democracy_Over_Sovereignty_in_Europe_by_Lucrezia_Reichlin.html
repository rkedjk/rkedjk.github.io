Here us an article which attempts to explain the increased polarity among US voters. It is quite likely something similar is going on in Europe. As only one side can win in an election the other side feel that democracy has failed them and do not accept democratic outcomes. To me this looks very much like the EU with incumbent liberals complaining about populists, rejecting anything other than their own view as stupid or ill-informed. Never the twain shall meet. Similarly there will never be federalism because it will never be accepted by all. So all this talk (propaganda in many cases) about trying to rationalise a blanket outcome aka enforcement by any other name, is just so much hot air In principle the EZ failed with GR and interest rate spread across the EZ. In principle Schengen failed with the migrant crisis. In both cases relatively small issues have shown how unresilient the central concept is. GR is 3% of EZ GDP. Migrants have been circa 1 million into a EU population of 500million. A robust system that had evolved under real conditions rather than being thought up by a few people would have shrugged off those sort of impacts http://uk.businessinsider.com/sociology-explains-polarization-politics-2017-3

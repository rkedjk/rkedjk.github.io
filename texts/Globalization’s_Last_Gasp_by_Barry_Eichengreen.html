Globalization’s Last Gasp by Barry Eichengreen

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/growth-before-globalization-by-barry-eichengreen-2016-11

The revolt against globalisation is a function of outrageous corporate rights in the TPP which can stop a country introducing things like limits on tobacco advertising without being sued and having to pay compensation to those lying merchants of death. It is a revolt against the outrageous increase in the remuneration of senior staff of large corporations while workers whose jobs are lost go backwards and many are never re-employed in any meaningful full time employment. It is a revolt against grwoing inequality as the very well off get tax cuts and accumulate more assets while many young adults cannot afford to bu a first home. It is a revolt against illegal immigrants taking jobs. It is a revolt against immigration straining and degrading services and infrastructure efficiency which has to get upgraded largely at the cost of the existing citizens. It is a revolt against loss of cultural identity through changes wrought by mass immigration where whole suburbs and regions are dramatically changed in "vibe" for the remaining long term residents It is a revolt against Central Banks not caring about house prices and other asset prices rising out of the reach of so many young adult citizens for the benefit of the wealthy but dramatically increasing the cost of a basic necessity, housing. It is about rising wages in China starting to change the growth dynamic and to reduce competitive advantage. It is about the impact of mercantilist polices in China, Germany and some other countries on employment types, security and rewards in other countries.

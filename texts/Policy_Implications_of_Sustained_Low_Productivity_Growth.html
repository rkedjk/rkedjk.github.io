Policy Implications of Sustained Low Productivity Growth The Peterson Institute for International Economics held a major research conference on the "Policy Implications of Sustained Low Productivity Growth" on November 9, 2017. Conference organizers Jeromin Zettelmeyer and Adam S. Posen assembled a group of leading policy economists to tackle the question of what the productivity slowdown would mean for the advanced economies over the next two decades, in terms of current institutions.

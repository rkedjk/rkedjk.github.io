Volcker’s Future as Obama Adviser Uncertain

https://dealbook.nytimes.com/2011/01/06/volckers-future-as-obama-adviser-uncertain/

WASHINGTON — The economic advisory panel that President Obama established at the start of his term is set to expire in a month, and its future — and that of its chairman, Paul A. Volcker — remains unclear. The panel, the President’s Economic Recovery Advisory Board, was established on Feb. 6, 2009, with a two-year mandate. That mandate could be extended by an executive order. So far, the president has not given a clear indication of his plans, but it appears that he intends to do so on Friday when he announces a new director of the National Economic Council and discusses the monthly unemployment figures. Reuters reported on Wednesday that Mr. Volcker, a former Federal Reserve chairman, intended to step down as chairman of the panel. Asked about the Reuters report, a spokesman for Mr. Volcker would not confirm it. “All we can say is that the President’s Economic Recovery Advisory Board executive order was for two years and that is up on Feb. 6, and there has been no official announcement that the president will extend the Recovery Board and what its official mission would be,” the spokesman said. “In terms of Mr. Volcker’s involvement, whether he is chairman of the Perab or not, he remains available to advise the president when he wants it.” The 17-member board was created to solicit ideas and information, provide analysis and “report directly to the president” on policies to promote growth and stabilize the financial system. But aside from Mr. Volcker, who was an active voice in the debates last year over how to overhaul Wall Street regulations, the board has not had a particularly high profile. The board is bipartisan. Its members include Richard L. Trumka, the president of the A.F.L.-C.I.O.; Roger W. Ferguson Jr., a former vice chairman of the Federal Reserve; William H. Donaldson, a former chairman of the Securities and Exchange Commission; the economists Martin S. Feldstein, Christina D. Romer and Laura D’Andrea Tyson; and executives like Jeffrey R. Immelt of General Electric and James W. Owens of Caterpillar.

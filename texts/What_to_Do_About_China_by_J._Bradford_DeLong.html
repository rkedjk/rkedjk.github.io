What to Do About China? by J. Bradford DeLong

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/china-america-relations-trump-diplomatic-weakness-by-j-bradford-delong

J. Bradford DeLong says Trump has the wrong strategy dealig with an ambitious China. Getting tough and imposing tariffs bring him only short-term victory while inflicting lasting damage on the US manufacturing. Trump is to blame for the total mess – America’s standing in the world has plummeted catastrophically since he took office. Global confidence in US leadership under him has fallen to a new low. The US now ranks below China in worldwide approval ratings, according to the 2018 Gallup poll. In a new cold war with China over trade deficits and dominance in technology, the US is on its own, because Trump has pushed away allies and punished them like enemies, while pandering to dictators and taking the word of foreign adversaries over US intelligence agencies. It suits Putin that Trump attacks international institutions – like the UN and NATO – doing long-term damage to alliances as well as crucial relationships and systems across the globe that the US built after World War II to secure universal peace and stability. The author says, if ever the US seeks to “reclaim” the global standing it once had, it must get its act together and “look inward, not abroad.” To start with, Americans need to fix their country’s broken system in politics that has led to hyper-partisanship and gridlock in Congress. They need to improve the healthcare sector, address social inequality and heal social conflicts. Upgrading the infrastructure, investing in high-tech sector and new energy sources, and welcoming talents from all over the world will help boost the economy. As long as Trump remains in office, the above mentioned goals would remain unmet. The spineless Republicans, instead of defending their values, they have succumbed to sycophancy, doing Trump’s bidding. They backed George W. Bush’s disastrous wars, which had bled the country’s economy. Today they stand by Trump, although he won the 2016 election thanks to Russian meddling and engaged in impeachable conduct, according to Robert Mueller’s report. Critics believe there is hope that the US could still “become what it would have been if Al Gore had won the 2000 presidential election, if Hillary Clinton had defeated Trump, and if the Republican party had not abandoned its patriotism.” The pre-Trump America was in decline, but still commanded the world’s respect under Barack Obama, and would have wielded “more than enough diplomatic power to forge a constructive and strategically sound compact with a rising China.” It will take years before the international community to trust the post-Trump America again. Thanks to its robust economy, its vibrant civil society, and a long history of grassroots movements, the US will survive Trump’s disastrous presidency. Although many Americans support tougher policies in dealing with China, they certainly do not contemplate fighting a conventional war with Beijing. The author says, a “great-power conflict “ could correspond to what the Prussian general Carl von Clausewitz called “politics by other means”. Since nobody knows what an impact such a conflict would have on the rest of the world, it would be a “folly to pursue politics by any means other than politics itself.” War as Clausewitz said, has its own logic, and once started cannot be easily stopped. We should never underestimate the terrible vagaries of the two world wars of the last century.

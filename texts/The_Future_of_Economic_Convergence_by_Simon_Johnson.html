The Future of Economic Convergence by Simon Johnson

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/future-of-economic-convergence-by-simon-johnson-2018-10

By 2050 there will be 5 billion consumers in Asia and 2 billion in Africa. Meanwhile EU and North America together will only amount to 1 billion. So if the rest of the World comes even close to Western levels of productivity (and why wouldn't they?) then a big shift in relative economic power is inevitable. We need to grow up and stop thinking about loss of American hegemony as a bad thing. A century ago the British Empire ruled or controlled trade across two thirds of the World. We were the America of our day, and of course Americans took great glee in supplanting us, after the 1930s. Yet although UK dominance declined, even the poorest citizens of the UK today live a vastly better life than her richest elites did when we ruled the World. What's more, in terms of soft power, British ideas and talent (our #SocialCapital) still have influence around the World way beyond our relative economic size. The 21st century will not belong to Asia, even as they rise inevitably to economic dominance. Progress belongs to the people, and we will all (even Americans) be far better off in a World of economic convergence. The real challenge for The Global Race is to evolve beyond our colonial inheritance of "Them And Us" thinking, and finally recognise the truth - that there is only one "us". Robert P Bruce, author "The Global Race"

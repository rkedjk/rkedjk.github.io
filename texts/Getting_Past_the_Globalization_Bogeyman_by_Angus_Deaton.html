Getting Past the Globalization Bogeyman by Angus Deaton

https://www.project-syndicate.org/onpoint/getting-past-the-globalization-bogeyman-by-angus-deaton-2017-01

I thjink this article imisses the polint (along with most 'market economics' theory from Marshall onward we have has pumped into us). It is advice and skill that create wealth, not ownership. The problem is the concentration of ownership in ever fewer hands and the increasing casualisation of labour - the system functions only by creating 'necessary poverty' that makes life dependent on the say-so of the self-interest of wealthy others. I suggest the need is not to rediscuss 'equality' but to push for shared ownership - no-one should work for any organisation they do not own in part. In that way we can see that the robot-production question is a mere red herring - once those production systems are owned mor equally and sustain leisure and income for more than a few big owners ther 'problem' ceases. We pay amazon, microsoft etc for the service they provide - not so that an owner can dole out billions to his favourite charities or to money-making take-over schemes. Nor is the 'third world' argument more than a pretence. Stable expectations of income and decent self-worth are destroyed as much by monetsing village life and wrenching people into scrap-yards, subsistence workshops and rubbish dumps as much as by the comparable disaster inflicted on the casualised 'white working class'. Villagers normally don't want to 'work' in the sense of joining a capitalist-run labour force. Their lives have first to ruined by poll-tax, rent-demands, land seizures, forced labour before they are open to the supposed 'marvells' of minority owned production-systems and uncontrolled reproduction to generate population excess and the necessary amount of deprivation for the system to 'motivate' them. I challenge the Professor to demonstrate from classical economics of individual satisfaction-maximisation any case for arguing that a man content in his cottage, grannies cider-apple orchard and a ragged coat increases his level of happiness by being shifted to line-production or mining etc. against his will as a result of finacialisation and impoverihment.

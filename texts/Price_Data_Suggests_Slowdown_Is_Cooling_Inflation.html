Price Data Suggests Slowdown Is Cooling Inflation

https://www.nytimes.com/2008/05/15/business/15econ.html?_r=1&ref=business&oref=slogin

Consumer prices edged up slightly in April, but far more slowly than many economists had expected, the government reported Wednesday, lending credence to the view that a slowing economy is applying the brakes to inflation. The data offered a measure of vindication to the Federal Reserve, which has been lowering interest rates in a bid to spur the economy amid fears of a recession, even as some economists have argued that this surge of cheap credit will worsen rising prices. The figures released by the Labor Department showed that consumer prices increased 0.2 percent in April, and 3.9 percent compared with the previous year. Prices grew by 0.1 percent when food and energy were excluded  about half what most economists forecast  and 2.3 percent compared with the previous year. This so-called core inflation rate is what the Fed tends to focus on as it adjusts interest rates, lowering them to encourage economic activity and raising them to snuff out rising prices.

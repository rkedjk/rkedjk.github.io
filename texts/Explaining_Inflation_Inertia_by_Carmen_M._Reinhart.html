Explaining Inflation Inertia by Carmen M. Reinhart

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/explaining-inflation-inertia-japan-argentina-by-carmen-reinhart-2019-05

Economists have not yet woken up to a fundamental change in the structure of the world economy. This change is the very large amount of savings made every year which is vastly in excess of the capacity of entrepreneurs to utilise it. Low interest rates should have the effect of reducing savings but this ignores the individual properties of the savers. They do not need to consume; they merely wish to preserve their capital. Integrate this into the analysis of the global economy and all becomes clear. There are few remedies; the easiest and most effective is confiscatory taxes on savers who will not spend. However most political systems are controlled or heavily influenced by the very rich (the savers who will not spend) so this remedy is unlikely to be employed. Consider the eventual outcome of a system of excessive saving where the corrective of reduced interest does not reduce saving. My prediction is a long term trend of rising negative interest rates; which, I suppose, will be a form of taxation. Pension funds take note!

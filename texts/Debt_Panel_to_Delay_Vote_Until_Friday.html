Debt Panel to Delay Vote Until Friday

https://thecaucus.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/11/30/debt-panel-to-delay-vote-until-friday/?partner=rss&emc=rss

The chairmen of President Obama’s debt-reduction commission say they will release a final plan by Wednesday morning, technically meeting the president’s deadline, but they delayed the panel’s vote until Friday to give members time to consider the far-reaching tax and spending proposals. Talks with people familiar with the panel’s deliberations indicate that the 18-member commission is far from consensus. The two chairmen and perhaps three appointees who are not elected officials are said to be in general agreement, but the 12 members of Congress are described as split along party lines – the six Republicans opposed to tax increases and the six Democrats to reductions from future health-care and Social Security benefits. “We will meet the deadline of Dec. 1,” the Republican co-chairman, former Senator Alan K. Simpson told reporters on Capitol Hill on Tuesday afternoon, but the vote will be on Friday. Erskine B. Bowles, the Democratic co-chairman and former chief of staff to President Bill Clinton, said, “We just finished the plan; we’re putting the final touches on it now.” It would not be fair, he added, “to not give people a chance to review it before they’re asked to vote.” The chairmen then left for another appointment with a commission member. Since Monday they have been meeting individually with each member to review changes made over the Thanksgiving break to the chairmen’s draft plan, based on members’ input at earlier commission sessions, and to lobby for support. Mr. Bowles said the ultimate plan would reduce projected deficits by 2020 by no less than $3.8 trillion. That was the bottom line for the comprehensive draft plan that he and Mr. Simpson introduced early in November as the basis for deliberations. Despite the low expectations for a commission agreement, Mr. Bowles said, “Al and I couldn’t be happier with where we are in the process regardless of how the vote turns out. I think we’ve won and we’ve won big: The era of deficit denial in Washington is over.” Mr. Obama supported the chairmen’s decision to delay a vote, according to a White House spokeswoman, Amy Brundage. “The President established this bipartisan commission to tackle our medium and long-term fiscal situation, and we believe they are taking this mandate seriously and working together in a constructive way,” Ms. Brundage said in a statement. The president, she added, “looks forward to hearing their recommendations this week.”

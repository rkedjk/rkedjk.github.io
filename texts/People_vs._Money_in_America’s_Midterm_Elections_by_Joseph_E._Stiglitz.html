People vs. Money in America’s Midterm Elections by Joseph E. Stiglitz

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/how-democrats-can-win-midterms-by-joseph-e-stiglitz-2018-10

half a century ago this planet had less than half as many people as it has today. In some places in the world, it had quarter as many people as it has today. disregarding economic ,and particularly demographic, realities in favor of free market ideologies brought the right to the point of becoming a fascist menace to the world. Facing social consequences of their policies, the right discovered the political effectiveness of scapegoating, and the rest may very well become (a very dark) history. the left though, is doing at best marginally better, or perhaps not better at all. It is true that the political pressure of market fundamentalism caused the left to shift into economic positions that would have been once called "center right". Perhaps to compensate, the left developed it's own library of fanatical, yet wholly counterproductive positions which attempt to manipulate society through propaganda. Excesses of political correctness are well known, an effective fodder for right wing propaganda, no need to belabor. A more insidious transformation is abuse of science as a political prop in service of extreme environmentalism. Unfounded claims of "human caused climate change", whipping up fears of nuclear energy, genetically modified crops, this is all part and parcel of present day left wing politics. Actual economic realities are pretty stark. The days when a high school dropout in America had a privilege of high paying, unionized auto job, paying enough to support a good life on a single income, even retire into a second home in Florida, are over, and over for good. Anyone trying to revive these good old days is a fraud, or worse, an idiot. Much larger world population is competing with this auto worker for scarce resources and putting downward pressure on his achievable living standard. This is the actual reality. The only way to slow, or reverse, this slide is technological progress that develops substitutes for scarce resources. As a society we do not have the luxury of replacing fossil fuels with solar panels and windmills, this is economically unfeasible. They can be a supplement, but not a replacement. Nuclear power and electrification of transportation could be a very effective supplement, perhaps bordering on replacement. Things I do not know about may come along, but they are not realistic here and now. The burning question is this: can markets deliver the transformation, or will markets simply balance supply and demand through widespread poverty and lock us down in the state of permanently unequal society? Is government intervention harmful, or actually necessary to nudge markets towards economic evolution? These are old questions but now becoming truly pressing as population pressures limit the array of choices. To rebuild the political center, people on the right and on the left have to make an effort to abandon some of their most cherished assumptions. The present day noise of ideological warfare waged through propaganda campaigns is not encouraging. In the past, horrific wars provided a universal reset button. Today, the next horrific war, fought with nukes, might be the last great war of the western civilization. This may be the final test. Can societies actually learn from the past? If Mr Stiglitz wants to be a part of the new political center, he will step back, take a deep breath, and ask: "which of my cherished assumptions do I need to drop, or at least dispassionately confront with reality". If enough people, on both sides, do it, there may still be hope, but time is definitly running out.

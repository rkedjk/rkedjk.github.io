When Will Tech Disrupt Higher Education? by Kenneth Rogoff

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/tech-disruption-of-higher-education-by-kenneth-rogoff-2018-02

I've been using LCMS technology in my language courses for ten years, and while it enables me to save a lot of time in marking and grading homework, i.e. textbook exercises, and administering exams, it is pretty much an online application of Skinner's learning machine. It can't handle anything much more complicated than the usual multiple choice or true/false answer question types. Even fill in the blanks types questions present considerable difficulties, as students frequently find the spaces are too small to see what they are writing. So you are limited in the kind of tests you can offer to what can be machine graded. Otherwise, what is the point? I did find online forum software useful for a debate class (for second language learners) as they could prepare their arguments and rebuttals beforehand, and I could have a written record of each students' contributions. But it is what goes on in the classroom that really counts as a learning experience. However, for debates conducted in the students' first language, in which it is paramount to think on one's feet, using the forum would be counterproductive. So my sense is that the tech is more of a convenience for the instructor, particularly for adjuncts with large course loads, like myself, who otherwise haven't the time to keep track of several hundred students at multiple institutions. I can easily distribute distribute materials for the students to print out themselves, rather than spend time I don't have making hard copies for in-class distribution.

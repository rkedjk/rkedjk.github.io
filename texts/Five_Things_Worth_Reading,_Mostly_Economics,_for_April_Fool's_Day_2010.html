Walt Mossberg: iPad... The Final Frontier... Mossberg: "The iPad is much more than an e-book or digital periodical reader, though it does those tasks brilliantly... it's far more than just a big iPhone.... It's qualitatively different, a whole new type of computer that, through a simple interface, can run more-sophisticated, PC-like software than a phone does, and whose large screen allows much more functionality.... But, because the iPad is a new type of computer, you have to feel it, to use it, to fully understand it and decide if it is for you, or whether, say, a netbook might do better. So I've been using my test iPad heavily.... I found the iPad a pleasure to use, and had less and less interest in cracking open my heavier ThinkPad or MacBook. I probably used the laptops about 20% as often as normal.... My verdict is that... the iPad can indeed replace a laptop for most data communication, content consumption and even limited content creation, a lot of the time. But it all depends on how you use your computer..." However, the study does not control for at least one other variable.... The district that received the largest amount of stimulus funding in the 4th Quarter of 2009... is California's 5th... This, of course, makes perfect sense. A lot of stimulus funds are distributed to state agencies, which... then... The 357 districts that are not home to a state capital received... $48 billion.... The other piece of the puzzle, of course, is that state capitals are much more likely to elect Democrats to Congress" Mike Schramm: Wolfram Alpha lowers iPhone app price, will give refunds Schramm: "Well here's an interesting twist on the Wolfram Alpha saga -- after initially releasing their "knowledge system" on the iPhone and then pulling it and trying to go premium with a $50 iPhone app, they've now reversed their position once again.... Wolfram Alpha seems to be cognizant of their mistaken decision to try and go premium.... It's great that everyone will have access to the app again (and we're sure they'll see a lot more usage as a result), but it does say something about the App Store when an app with as much function as Wolfram Alpha can't find a premium selling point. Hopefully they'll find some other way to monetize and keep the service up and running. Assuming this isn't actually a bad April Fool's story (the mobile site is back up and running now, even though the app price isn't scheduled to change until Sunday), we'll see how Wolfram Alpha does with this new philosophy." Justin Fox: Wresting the Economic Debate Away from the Economists Fox: "I... just spent time with a bunch of historians... wondering how they could become more like economists.... I... ended up ... in a long discussion with the 30-or-so historians... on hand about why economists had gained so much influence.... One answer I offered was that economists had managed a remarkable balancing act between making the guts of their work... incomprehensible ... thus... impressive ... while continuing to offer... straightforward conclusions.... An academic history paper... is often... semi-comprehensible jargon that neither impresses... nor offers... conclusions.... I heard another explanation from the historians... that... only the tiniest minority of academic historians concern themselves anymore with matters of economic policy (or diplomacy, or war, or politics in the big-picture sense). The discipline has moved mostly to the study of identity (gender, race, etc.) and culture, ceding territory to the economists and political scientists..."

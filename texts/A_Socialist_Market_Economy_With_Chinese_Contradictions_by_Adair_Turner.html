Show paragraph I vote for the pessimistic scenario. The Communist attempt to unify China into a single nation originated in the north. The northern Chinese system is dependent upon the application of labor -- mass labor -- to whatever problems arise. Politically, the result is a system in which the average person has no voice whatsoever, and in which local authorities exercise autonomy so great that it risks bringing the system down. Once a Chinese leader succeeds in commanding the entirety of China, he then must begin the even longer and more painful slog of purging all those who have visions that clash with his own. Considering how fractured China is normally and how many power centers there are, this typically requires all of the leader's attention. That was as true for the emperors of old as it is for the current premier, Xi Jinping. Thus, true political reform would at this point be like letting the genie out of the bottle, threatening loss of the Party's control and leading to Xi's greatest fear: the disunion of China.

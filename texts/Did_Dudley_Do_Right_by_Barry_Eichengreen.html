Did Dudley Do Right? by Barry Eichengreen

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/dudley-new-york-fed-2020-election-by-barry-eichengreen-2019-09

I can't agree with Barry's take on Dudley's comments more completely. But in many ways he's made it more complicated than is necessary. The Fed can eliminate all the hand-wringing around political considerations and appearances to be capitulating to the administration about trade policy, by completely ignoring everything other than their Congressional mandates: price stability; full employment. By doing so, they can react to any policy position taken by the president and other policy makers, and just adjust monetary policy and rates to maximize their two mandates. They don't need to send a message. They have no responsibility as regards influencing elections etc. They just need to adhere to both those mandates and adjust policy accordingly. Clearly, they should be speaking in private and giving their views to the president as to what his trade policies and their implications and other political decisions could have on the state of the economy and the job at hand that the FOMC has before them. But beyond that, I don't think they need to worry about any message they're sending, or worse yet being proactive about shaping or otherwise influencing political decisions or elections through altering monetary policy to achieve anything other than price stability and full employment.

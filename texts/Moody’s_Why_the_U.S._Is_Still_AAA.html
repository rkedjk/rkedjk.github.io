Moody’s: Why the U.S. Is Still AAA

https://economix.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/08/08/moodys-why-the-u-s-is-still-aaa/

Virtually unnoticed last week was that Moody’s kept a Triple A rating on the United States. On Monday, the credit rating agency released its “Weekly Credit Outlook” publication discussing the decision. Whether by coincidence or not, the stock market turned up just after 11 a.m., just as the wires carried headlines from that. (Why it is news that Moody’s has not changed its mind in the past week is for someone else to explain.) The United States, Moody’s says, has “unmatched access to financing, meaning that the U.S. government can support higher debt levels than other governments.” Somebody tell Washington. Greece has a debt crisis. The United States had a debt limit crisis. It can, if it wishes, do something to try to avert a new plunge into global recession, which has become a real fear.

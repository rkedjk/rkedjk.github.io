Stop Inflating the Inflation Threat by J. Bradford DeLong

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/us-inflation-flat-phillips-curve-by-j-bradford-delong-2019-10

The elicitation for the rate of unemployment has been changed since Clinton! But when looking at the rate of unemployment plus marginally attached workers and those woking part time because of economic reasons the rate of unemployment is still at 7%! Having said that it seems that even Professors didn't notice the severe changes in the world economy since 1989! The world doesn't consist of North America, Europe, Australia, New Zealand and Japan anymore. Cheap goods from Asia, the increase of capacity in goods and services, has not only put pressure on the prices of goods and services but also on wages and therefore on inflation. As we know wages are stagnant for decades now. In the seventies the average US consumer spent 15% on food (in the 50's even 22%!). Today it is about 6.6%. Prices and spending has changed and therefore the basket for calculating the CPI must have changed dramatically! As a professor one should know that you can't compare apples with oranges. Even the product range has increased dramatically in the past decades. Today's economy has much more surrogates and substitutes where consumers can shift and optimize spending and "control" inflation. Furthremore it seems that most economists today forgot that only money in circulation creates demand and therefore influencing prices. But if one includes art, stock marekts, luxury goods etc. into the CPI one would see that we indeed have high inflaiton in sectors where cheap money flows first (see Ockham). But those sectors do usually not effect the average Joe directly and CPI. Then we have much more sophisticated payment methods today then we had 50 years ago. Velocity of money therefore dropped rapidly and so did inflation of course. Excess money "disappers" right away since many transactions are cleared by money transfers from one bank to the other (or from the credit institutions). Today it is harder for excess money entering the economic cycle, since money not in use will just stay on the bank account and therefore not create any demand. There are also fewer unbanked people. Then the personal savings rate increased from 2.2% in 2005 to 8.3% in 2019! This does not only suppresse inflation but it is acutally increasing the value of the dollar and causing deflationary pressure. Furthermore there is a distinct difference between inflation due to increasing money supply (which is held as excess reserves at the FED) and for example increasing demand or decreasing supply of goods and services. The US consumer today has not the power to increase demand for goods and services so it would substantially increase prices, since wages didn't really increase since decades. And in contrast to 50 years ago the money of the consumer is today spread on a huge variaty of goods and services that even didn't exist 50 years ago and that makes it actually quite hard to increase demand in all those products at once. This puts deflationary pressure on all those new goods and services. Interesting that a lot of economists are looking suddenly for new economic theories (see MMT) when they can't explain or see what's happening right in front of their eyes and aren't even noticing the most severe transfomations in the economy. It is as these economists are still looking on horse carriage wheras the economy runs on a car with combustion engine or should I say EV. To apply the Phillips curve in such a blunt way without considering the above is a pity.

How Central-Bank Independence Dies by Kenneth Rogoff

https://www.project-syndicate.org/onpoint/how-central-bank-independence-dies-by-kenneth-rogoff-2019-05?barrier=accesspaylog

Where to start, with Rogoff's views and proposal... Firstly, his proposal of forcing the implementation of negative bank rates means that deflation would lead to extreme financialization of the economy, always benefiting more finance, having even the banks run behind speculative dealers and finance, becoming de facto shadow banks. He is so absorbed by his concerns over wholesale finance that he suggests retail and consumer agents to have no safe access to savings and be forced to provide them to speculators; as if speculators are doing anything useful with the extensive borrowing they are engaged in! This will only increase the motivation of speculators to buy and sell for no reason, other than to distort prices to the point they make profit out of anything traded and cause more frequent crisis! Secondly, his requirement that there are no more cash but only electronic payments and that, electronic or not, cash will be subject to negative rates, in times of crisis and deflation, means exacerbation of the dependence of economic agents from the payment system, which will be used by central banks as a vehicle for rent extraction. Effectively, this means that money (currency) will move, from being a means of payment toward being a means of financial rent-seeking and all consumers and small firms will have to compete with high finance or pay its rents, in a crisis! In other words, in a crisis, the bailout will be forced on the real economy, as a mandatory rent, embedded in the monetary system! In other words, no scarcity is needed to balance elasticity in the monetary system, as long as central banks support the oligopoly of high finance. And this is supposed to *save* central bank independence!!! Thirdly, although Rogoff purports to support debt cancellation and provides hints in this direction, he cares not to elaborate on this fiscal realm issues, as if the problems of central bank independence, with which he is so concerned, is a technical problem and would not go away if the post-recession effects on the economy disappeared. In fact, it appears that central bank independence is a voodoo technical problem, whose solutions are not even meant to address deflation! Thus, even with actual technical solutions that make some economic sense, like injecting debt-free money to the economy to repay debts and reduce debt-levels, when it comes to criticising helicopter money or MMT, Rogoff sees possibility of political opposition and lack of central bank authority to equal distribution of helicopter money to citizens, purports limited understanding of MMT and invents variants of the actual proposals that would introduce finance before debt-free money hits the real economy. For helicopter money he finds it "unclear" that it would trigger inflation (reasonable since he invented a variant of the actual proposal where "debt-free" money comes from the government issuing debt bought by the central bank, but then repays the debt, effectively removing the money from circulation) and neglects to tell us about MMT, how much "soaring inflation" could result if long-term government debt of only 1% of GDP were bought by the central bank, which created new "reserves [that *obviously*] do not pay interest"; "inflation would soar" no matter the amount of monetary stimulus, in Rogoff's dismissive "arguments". Rogoff is clearly arguing all this to... make an appearance as an economist and to demonstrate ability to "prove" anything with economically "sound" arguments; that is either to remain eligible to receive research funding, or to find a more profitable job in policymaking...

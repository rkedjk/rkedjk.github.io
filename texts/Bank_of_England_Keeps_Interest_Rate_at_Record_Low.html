Bank of England Keeps Interest Rate at Record Low

https://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/06/business/international/bank-of-england-keeps-interest-rate-at-record-low.html?partner=rss&emc=rss

LONDON — Britain’s central bank on Thursday decided to keep its benchmark interest rate unchanged at a record low of 0.5 percent as the country’s economy continues to improve faster than those on the European continent. The Bank of England also voted to hold its program of economic stimulus at 375 billion pounds, or $614 billion. Loose monetary policy since 2009 and improving confidence among businesses and consumers have helped the British economy to grow 0.8 percent in the third quarter from the second quarter, more than Germany, France or the euro zone as a whole. “Growth momentum in the U.K. is strong,” Jens Larsen, the chief European economist at RBC Capital Markets in London, said before the announcement. “The public deficit is likely to shrink much faster than initially thought. The government strategy seems to pay off.” The European Central Bank was also expected to leave its main interest rate at 0.25 percent on Thursday after a surprise cut from 0.5 percent last month to head off what many economists warned could become a long period of stagnation. The bank was to announce the rate decision at 1:45 p.m. in Frankfurt.

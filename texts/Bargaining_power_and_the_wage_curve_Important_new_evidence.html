Bargaining power and the wage curve: Important new evidence

https://www.washingtonpost.com/posteverything/wp/2016/02/08/bargaining-power-and-the-wage-curve-important-new-evidence/

You may have expected a straight, negative correlation: the bigger the gap, the slower the wage growth. Instead, you see that the curve flattens out at around 2 percent wage growth with a gap of around 5 percent of the potential labor force. That’s because of “downward nominal wage rigidity”: Employers might not give you much of a raise in weak economies, but, for morale reasons, they hesitate to cut your nominal wages. They will let inflation erode them, but they don’t often like to cut them. (Okay, I take it back: even this simple relationship isn’t all that simple.)

Ezra Klein Falls Victim to the Church of the Savvy: Campaigns Are By and Large Not Composed of Amoral Hired Guns

http://delong.typepad.com/sdj/2013/08/ezra-klein-falls-victim-to-the-church-of-the-savvy-campaigns-are-by-and-large-not-composed-of-amoral-hired-guns.html

The old Bob Woodward line was always: Everybody is posturing all the time in public, but I talk to them off-the-record and get the truth, so I am the unique person who knows what is going on, and so I am the person who you need to pay attention to. Ezra Klein: Mitch McConnell’s campaign manager doesn’t love him. So?: As part of his ongoing effort to shore up his right flank, Mitch McConnell tapped Jesse Benton, Rand Paul’s 2010 campaign manager, to lead his reelection effort… a marriage of political convenience…. Now, thanks to a surreptitiously recorded phone conversation between Benton and an ex-friend, we’ve got Benton dissing McConnell on the record: I am, well between you and me, I’m sort of holding my nose for two years, ‘cause what we’re doing here is going to be a big benefit to Rand in 16 so. That’s my long vision. This has sent shudders of glee through the political class because it’s August and politics right now is boring. But there’s nothing here. 1) It’s hardly news that campaigns are composed of hired guns, many of whom don’t love, and sometimes don’t even like, the people they work for. Benton’s comments are on the milder end of that spectrum. 2) These were, remember, private comments. People deserve to have their private comments to people they thought were their friends left off-the-record…. Some campaign hack admitting he doesn’t love his boss is not a good reason…. McConnell’s tweet making light of the situation (and taking a gratuitous and nonsensical swipe at Obamacare) basically seems like the right response here. In actual fact, no. Nobody gets into campaign politics to make money. Some lose their way and become amoral hacks, but most do not. Most campaign operatives who lie to journalists do so because of what they regard as a strong moral geas imposed on them by the requirement that they try to make America a better country. Certainly Jesse Benton is not a hired gun, not a hack: he is somebody who has staked years of his life on a quest that seemed hopeless to swing the Republican Party to the right and to advance the career of Rand Paul. He is still working to advance the career of Rand Paul. And, if you are a candidate, you want your campaign managers to be the most competent people you can find who strongly believe in you. People who believe in you will work for you rather than take the million opportunities they have to cut corners and advance their own standing quite possibly at your expense. That McConnell found it to his advantage to hire a campaign manager who did not believe in him rather than hire the most competent person he could find who did believe in him tells us something important about both McConnell and about who believes in him. The Church of the Savvy--and McConnell--has an interest in making light of the situation. For the rest of us, it's a relevant piece of information.

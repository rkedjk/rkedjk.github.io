Housing Bubble Déjà Vu by Mark Roe

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/us-housing-market-regulation-mortgage-pools-by-mark-roe-2017-03

Stating that the implosion of the housing bubble created havoc in the MBS market amounts to saying that Kennedy killed the magic bullet. The 2008 crisis was lit because of a growing dollar shortage in European banks, it topped circa 800 billion ultimately. When collateral had to be sold to cover for the mismatch, banks started to sell MBS, as well as other assets. When the sell of collateral accelerated, bidders started to look more in detail what they were buying. And it is then when it was "discovered" that AAA bonds were stuffed with subprime mortgages. Bids for MBS disappeared. And MBS cratered. That is why it all went so abrupt. The ensuing credit crisis popped the housing bubble. The overnight market for MBS is still today a huge source or concern. Ultra short term deposits finance long term maturing projects (aka mortgages). Will we ever learn something ?

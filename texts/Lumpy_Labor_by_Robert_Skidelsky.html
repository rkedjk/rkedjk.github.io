Per Skidelsky -- and his: " ... many workers will have to accept a pittance to find any employment, and become increasingly dependent on social transfers as market-clearing wages fall below the subsistence level?" I believe our economy has happily grown so much more productive since LBJ announced his War on Poverty that no market-clearing rate need fall below subsistence. Happy US? Not until our labor market is reformed so that labor is actually able to extract the max the consumer is willing to pay -- rather than be stuck with the minimum the boss can get away with paying (I call this a two-tier labor market). Instant start: $15 min wage -- shift all of 3.5% of GDP to bottom 45% earners. Any two people can earn subsistence and a bit more. * * "and a bit more": $11,000 silver plan for family of four (brill, p.346), $4,000 payroll taxes on $50,000, #15,000 rent and utilities -- and you haven't even put the dried beans in the pot to soak overnight to save on expensive canned. Ideal of course: across the board unionization -- gold standard: centralized bargaining. Right now 100,000 out of I estimate 200,000 Chicago, gang age males are in street gangs -- wont work for a min wage $3.50 lower than LBJ's, double per capita income later. So, market clearing wages below subsistence could long ago been eliminated as a practical outcome.

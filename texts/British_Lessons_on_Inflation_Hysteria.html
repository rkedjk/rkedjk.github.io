British Lessons on Inflation Hysteria

https://krugman.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/06/23/british-lessons-on-inflation-hysteria/?_php=true&_type=blogs&_r=0

Tim Duy tells us that “inflation hysteria is gripping Wall Street“; that matches my own observations. A modest uptick in core inflation has all the usual suspects declaring that the Fed will/must tighten now now now. Meanwhile, Janet Yellen is looking at wages, which are going nowhere, and sitting tight. There is actually a recent historical parallel, which I’m surprised hasn’t been pointed out: Britain in 2011, which had a much more pronounced inflationary bulge than anything we’re seeing here: Photo But wages were going nowhere, and the Bank of England refused to tighten. I remember being given a great deal of grief for defending this policy, with many accusations of being blind to the terrible inflationary risks. In fact, however, it turned out that the inflation bump was a blip, that you can’t have an inflation takeoff with quiescent wages. And the BoE was right to stay the course. So Yellen has history on her side.

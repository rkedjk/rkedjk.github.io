The Affordable Care Act’s Second Act by Laura Tyson & Lenny Mendonca

https://www.project-syndicate.org/commentary/four-steps-to-strengthen-obamacare-by-laura-tyson-and-lenny-mendonca-2017-10

Medicare for All has greater appeal and savings. There is something perverse about parasitical insurance companies hijacking costs, basically screwing everyone in sight, and this needs elimination. The simple fact that we pay about double what we should be paying should be enough to prompt immediate radical reform. Total expenses are reduces by almost a third under a M.f.A program, the Physician for a National Health Plan show data indicating reduction of 20% to 33%, by $600 billion to $1 trillion. It covers everyone, which ACA doesn't. It also creates a standard of medical care as a human right - join the civilized world. The International Federation of Health Plans has a run-down of comparative prices for about 100 surgeries, medications and procedures, and the U.S. is always around double what other nations are paying -- e.g. an angiogram costs $1,191 on average in the U.S., in Switzerland, Australia and Spain around $200. Go figure. The USCensus Supplemental Poverty Measure reports that poverty rate would decrease by 3.4%, to about 10%, if Medical Out of Pocket expenses were eliminated. About 45% of U.S. adults carry a medical debt, says the Federal Reserve report on Household Well-Being. The arguments for eliminating private insurance are enormously persuasive.

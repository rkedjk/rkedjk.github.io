Pain in the Periphery

https://krugman.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/12/30/pain-in-the-periphery/

Via Philip Lane, a nice paper comparing the crisis responses of Ireland, Iceland, and Latvia (pdf). It develops at greater length some themes I’ve been trying to lay out, notably the fact that nobody has succeeded in achieving large nominal wage cuts. If I have a slight complaint, it is that the paper doesn’t mention the distinction between GDP and GNP, which is important for Ireland and makes the Irish experience look worse once you take it into account; and it also doesn’t highlight the likely extent to which the big productivity improvement that has brought down Irish unit labor costs is really just a compositional effect. The bottom line is that internal devaluation still looks like a very bad strategy.

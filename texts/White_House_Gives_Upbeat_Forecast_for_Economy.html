White House Gives Upbeat Forecast for Economy

https://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/14/business/economy/14jobs.html?ref=business

Jobs in health care and those requiring postsecondary education will show the biggest gains by 2016, according to a report released on Monday by the president’s Council of Economic Advisers. The report, titled “Preparing the Workers of Today for the Jobs of Tomorrow,” surveys previous research done on the parts of the labor force expected to grow most quickly. It then argues for ways to improve the educational system so that American workers can more readily adapt to an ever more skill-based economy. The report was a response, in part, to criticism of the president’s $787 billion economic stimulus plan. The administration contends its plan will create millions of jobs. The economy of 2016, the report says, will look very much like the economy of 2008 in terms of how workers will be distributed across major industries.

ECONOMY Bank to extend access to unlimited funds The European Central Bank will extend its offerings of unlimited cash and keep buying government bonds as it tries to ease tensions in money markets and fight the European debt crisis, the head of the bank said Thursday. "It's appropriate to continue to do what we've decided" on purchases of sovereign and corporate bonds, Jean-Claude Trichet said in Frankfurt, Germany, where the bank is based. Earlier, the central bank kept its benchmark interest rate at 1 percent. Trichet said the ECB plans to offer further help to banks struggling to raise cash in money markets.

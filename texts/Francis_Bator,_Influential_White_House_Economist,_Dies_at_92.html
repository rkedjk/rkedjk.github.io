Francis Bator, Influential White House Economist, Dies at 92

https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/20/obituaries/francis-bator-influential-white-house-economist-dies-at-92.html?partner=rss&emc=rss

Francis M. Bator, a White House economist who nudged the Johnson administration toward a closer collaboration with Western Europe while reconciling with the Soviet Union and its Communist satellites, died on Thursday in Dedham, Mass. He was 92. He had been struck by an automobile in January while crossing the street and never recovered, his son, Christopher, said. Professor Bator (pronounced BAT-or), who fled Hungary with his family in the face of encroaching fascism in 1939, was one of a cadre of White House advisers in the Kennedy and Johnson administrations who shuttled between academia and the White House, joining the likes of Arthur M. Schlesinger Jr., McGeorge Bundy and Walt W. Rostow. In Washington, Professor Bator proposed federal programs and policies that were later incorporated into President Lyndon B. Johnson’s War on Poverty. After leaving the administration in 1967, he became founding chairman of the public policy program at what is now the Harvard Kennedy School and director of studies at the school’s Institute of Politics. He retired as a professor in 1994.
